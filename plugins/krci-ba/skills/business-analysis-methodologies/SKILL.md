# Business Analysis Methodologies

Core principles and techniques that underpin every business analysis deliverable — requirements gathering, process analysis, business rules, and user journey mapping. Apply these principles when executing any of the other skills in this plugin.

## How to Use

Apply the principles in `references/analysis-methodologies.md` throughout analysis work:

- Select the techniques appropriate to the deliverable and scope rather than applying all of them mechanically.
- Lead with stakeholder needs and business value; stay solution-agnostic until the problem is well understood.
- Keep traceability from business needs to documented requirements at all times.
- Base conclusions on data and evidence, and validate findings iteratively with stakeholders.

## Principle Summary

- **Stakeholder-centric**: identify all stakeholders; balance competing needs.
- **Requirements-driven**: trace every activity back to clear business objectives.
- **Process-oriented**: analyze activities as interconnected processes and systems.
- **Data-driven**: support recommendations with quantitative and qualitative evidence.
- **Continuous improvement**: challenge assumptions; recommend best practices.
- **Risk-aware**: identify, assess, and mitigate business, technical, and project risks.
- **Solution-agnostic**: understand the problem fully before prescribing solutions.
- **Communication excellence**: adapt to the audience; use visual models.
- **Collaborative facilitation**: build consensus across cross-functional teams.
- **Iterative and adaptive**: use feedback cycles; adapt to changing conditions.
