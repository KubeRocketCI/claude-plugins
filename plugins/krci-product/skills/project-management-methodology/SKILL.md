# Project Management Methodology

Core principles, process groups, and knowledge area guidance that underpin every project management deliverable — project charter, scope of work, project plan, risk register, and status report. Apply these principles when executing any of the other skills in this plugin.

## How to Use

Apply the principles in `references/project-management-methodology.md` throughout project management work:

- Select the techniques appropriate to the deliverable, phase, and project complexity rather than applying all of them mechanically.
- Lead with stakeholder needs and business value; align every artifact to the approved project objectives.
- Maintain traceability from business requirements to project artifacts at all times.
- Base planning on realistic estimates supported by data; use integrated change control for any baseline modifications.
- Apply earned value management (EVM) to objectively measure schedule and cost performance.

## Principle Summary

- **Stakeholder-focused**: engage stakeholders effectively throughout the project lifecycle.
- **Collaborative**: foster team collaboration and collective accountability.
- **Value-driven**: focus on outcomes and business value delivery, not just outputs.
- **Systems thinking**: recognize system interactions and interdependencies across knowledge areas.
- **Risk-aware**: identify, assess, and respond to threats and opportunities proactively.
- **Quality-embedded**: build quality into processes and deliverables from the start.
- **Adaptive and tailored**: select development approach and lifecycle appropriate to project context.
- **Leadership-oriented**: demonstrate leadership behaviors that motivate team performance.
- **Complexity-navigating**: apply adaptive and resilient practices when facing uncertainty.
- **Continuous improvement**: apply lessons learned; improve processes across the lifecycle.

## Process Group Reference

- **Initiating**: Charter, stakeholder identification, preliminary scope, governance setup.
- **Planning**: Integrated project plan, WBS, schedule, budget, quality, communications, risk, and procurement plans.
- **Executing**: Direct project work, manage stakeholder engagement, implement quality assurance.
- **Monitoring and Controlling**: Performance measurement (SPI, CPI), variance analysis, change control, risk monitoring, status reporting.
- **Closing**: Deliver final outputs, capture lessons learned, release resources, archive documents.
